IGN

GREAT The first season of Mike Judge’s HBO comedy Silicon Valley gave us one of the year’s best new show.
By Cliff Wheatley | JUNE 06, 2014

After a decent start in its first couple of episodes, Mike Judge’s Silicon Valley quickly gained steam to deliver one of the funniest comedies on TV this season. Following the misadventures of a tech start-up with no definitive direction and a cast of perverse, neurotic, and naïve slackers, Silicon Valley introduced us to a cast of characters that experienced real growth in the span of only eight episodes.

Season 1 was bold to introduce as many characters as it did; the cast ballooned quickly and there were a few causalities as a result (the Asian roommate, Big Head), but generally the main characters got the attention they deserved episode-to-episode. However, it’s worth noting that only Richard and Erlich – and to a lesser degree, Jared – really had actual arcs over the course of the season.

We got to see Richard grow from a timid and underachieving programmer to CEO, Erlich from annoying, overconfident coattail rider to valued (if still overconfident) promo man, and Jared from invisible perfunctory team member to acknowledged presence within the crew. In retrospect, the journeys of Richard and Erlich work quite well in context of the entire season, but early on it was a little tiresome to see them hit the same incompetent beats.

Dinesh and Gilfoyle were less developed, serving mainly to be rivals to each other and offer up a bulk of the gags, but their presence added a load of humor in every episode (Dinesh’s dilemma about sleeping with Gilfoyle’s girlfriend was a particular standout).
